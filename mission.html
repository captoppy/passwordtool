01 / Did you complete the mission?*
The mission was: ""
Required
Tell us what stopped you
02 / Overall Rating*
1 = really struggled, 5 = genuinely impressed
Pick a rating
03 / Friction Points*
Where did you get stuck, confused, or want to give up? At least one is required. Be specific — describe where you were and what happened. "The export button did nothing when I clicked it on the dashboard" is useful. "UX is bad" is not.
Ask yourself: What made me pause? What took longer than expected? What almost made me leave?
At least one friction point is required (20+ characters).
04 / Magic Moments optional
What worked better than expected? What made you think "oh, that's clever"? Builders need to know what to keep and double down on — don't skip this if something impressed you.
Ask yourself: What surprised me? What felt smooth? What would I show a friend?
05 / Expectation vs Reality optional but valuable
Based on the mission brief ("") — did the product match what you expected? Builders often don't realise their description is misleading — this is gold for fixing that.
e.g. "The description said 'one-click export' but I couldn't find it anywhere. I expected it to be front and centre."
06 / If you could make ONE change*
This is the most actionable thing you'll write. Not a wishlist — just the single change that would most improve this project right now. Force yourself to choose one.
Good answers name a specific screen, action, or moment. "Move the pricing link into the main nav — I missed it completely" beats "better navigation".
Required — name the single most important change (20+ characters).
07 / Your Overall Summary*
Imagine you're telling a friend who builds things: "I just tested this thing, here's the honest verdict." One to three sentences. Be direct. The builder is paying for this — make it count.
Good: "Core idea is solid and the UI is clean, but the onboarding drops you in the deep end — I had no idea what to do first. Fix that and it's genuinely useful."
Required — at least 40 characters. Be specific and direct.
08 / Would you use this again? optional
Pure gut check. If you said No, pick the closest reason — builders need to understand why someone wouldn't come back, not just that they wouldn't.
09 / Would you recommend this to the target user? optional
The builder described their target user as: —. Thinking specifically about that person — would you recommend it to them?
10 / First Impression paid review
The moment the page loaded — what was your gut reaction? Within the first 5 seconds, what did you understand (or not) about what this product does?
e.g. "Immediately understood it was a tool for developers. The headline was sharp. But the CTA was ambiguous — 'Get Started' didn't tell me what I was getting started with."
11 / Would you pay for this? paid review
Honest conversion check — not based on price, based on perceived value. Did this feel like something worth paying for?
